The Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA) has released Consumer Price Index (CPI) of goods in the Philippines with respect to 2018 prices. The following are the complete list of goods in the PSA report:

  • Food and Non-Alcoholic Beverages
  • Alcoholic Beverages and Tobacco
  • Clothing and Footwear
  • Housing, Water, Electricity, Gas and Other Fuels
  • Furnishings, Household Equipment and Routine Household Maintenance
  • Health
  • Transport
  • Information and Communication
  • Recreation, Sport and Culture
  • Education Services
  • Restaurants and Accommodation Services
  • Financial Services
  • Personal Care, and Miscellaneous Goods and Services

CPI is a metric used to reveal inflation and cost of living. An increase in CPI is indicative to an increase in price relative to a certain period of time. Last June 2025, PSA reports that the CPI of common goods rose to 127% relative to 2018 prices. In this page, we will discuss which goods are mostly affected and which ones are not to identify which ones are more prone to inflation and those that are more resistant to it. We will be using Python as a tool help us cluster our data and have a meaningful analysis.
